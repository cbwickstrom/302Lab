Nutrition Diagnosis - PES Format (2 pts)

Excessive energy intake R/T CABG, diet, family hx, sedentary, smoking AEB high levels of triglyceride at 250 mg/dl, total cholesterol at 240 mg/dl, and LDL at 153 mg/dl, and a low level of HDL at 37 mg/dl.

 

Nutrition Intervention (3 pts)   

1.    Meals and Snacks:

a.    Consume whole grains: whole meal or wholegrain bread or crackers, brown rice, whole meal pasta, quinoa, barley, rye, rolled oats, polenta & couscous

b.    At the minimum, meet the DRI of fruits and veggie intake

c.    Reduce saturated fat intake and incorporate small amounts of healthy fats and oils from nuts, seeds, avocado and oily fish

d.    Reduce sugary foods and beverages

e.    Utilize a reusable water bottle to promote consumption of fluid DRI and monitor water intake.

f.      Focus on nutrient-dense foods versus calorie-dense foods.

 

2.    Education:

a.    Importance of diet

b.    Understand how high low-density lipoprotein cholesterol affects health and risk for heart disease and stroke

 

3. Counseling: Common to experience psychosocial issues post-op—see therapist

 

4. Coordination of nutrition care: exercise physiologist for rehab, smoking cessation class, therapist

 

Goals:

  1. Normalize lipid profile within 3-6 months
  2. Meet kcal DRI within 1 week
  3. Incorporate more plant-based food sources starting today
  4. Participate in regular exercise within 1-2 weeks
  5. Drink sufficient fluids starting now
  6. Stop smoking as soon as possible

 

Monitoring and Evaluation (2pts)

  • Follow-up phone call within 1 week
  • Re-assess lipid profile in 3-6 months to determine if within normal ranges
  • Ask patient to complete a 3-day food record after a clinic visit
